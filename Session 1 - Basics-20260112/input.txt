In this comprehensive course, students will delve into the intricacies of computer networks and distributed systems, equipping themselves with a profound understanding of the technologies essential for their management and utilization. The curriculum is structured to foster a deep grasp of key concepts and practical skills necessary for navigating the complexities of modern network infrastructures. The journey begins with Module 1, which offers an immersive exploration of the principles and models underpinning distributed systems. Students will gain insights into the architecture of distributed systems, learning about processes, threads, virtualization, and scalability. Additionally, they will delve into essential protocols such as DNS and HTTP, alongside mastering communication techniques, socket programming, APIs, and remote procedure calls. Moreover, an introduction to the versatile Python programming language will empower students to apply their newfound knowledge in practical settings. Through engaging lab exercises, learners will have the opportunity to solidify their understanding of these foundational topics. Moving forward, Module 2 ventures into the realm of telecommunications, focusing on the diverse applications of distributed systems. Students will explore cutting-edge concepts such as cloud, edge, and fog computing, along with service orchestration methodologies. Furthermore, the course delves into emerging technologies like Software-Defined Networking (SDN) and Network Function Virtualization (NFV), enabling students to grasp their implications in modern telecommunications frameworks. With a blend of theoretical insights and hands-on experience, this course promises to equip students with the expertise needed to navigate and thrive in the dynamic world of computer networks and distributed systems.
